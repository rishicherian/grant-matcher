Summary of MSC Fisheries Funding Opportunities
The Marine Stewardship Council (MSC) offers funding through its Ocean Stewardship Fund to promote sustainable fisheries globally. This initiative dedicates 5% of annual royalties from MSC-certified product sales, plus third-party donations, to support innovative research and fisheries at various stages of sustainability. To date, nearly $13 million has been mobilized, with $8.8 million allocated to nearly 200 fisheries and projects aimed at protecting oceans and reducing environmental impacts.
Key Funding Program
Ocean Stewardship Fund: Provides grants for MSC-certified fisheries or those in improvement projects. The 2025 Call for Proposals is currently open. Download the full Call for Proposals document (PDF, 7 MB) for detailed guidelines.
Eligibility
Open to fisheries that are MSC-certified or actively participating in MSC improvement projects.
Application Process
Review and submit via the 2025 Call for Proposals document available on the page. Specific application instructions are outlined in the PDF.
Benefits
Financial support for research and implementation of sustainable practices.
Helps fisheries minimize marine environmental impacts and advance toward full certification.
Deadlines and Notes
The 2025 Call is open (no specific closing date mentioned on the page—check the PDF for updates).
Funding focuses on bold, impactful innovations in sustainability.

In the world of North East Atlantic (NEA) pelagic fisheries, October and November mark important annual milestones. Throughout October, we have shared updates on the Coastal States Annual Negotiations - when fishing nations meet to finalise catch shares and quota allocations for mackerel, blue whiting and Atlanto-Scandian herring (ASH). Meanwhile, each November, Coastal States representatives gather in London for the annual North East Atlantic Fisheries Commission (#NEAFC) meeting, where the focus shifts to the management of these stocks in international waters.
NEAFC is one of a global network of Regional Fisheries Management Organisations (#RFMOs) that oversee and manage fishing on the High Seas. NEAFC focuses on fishing in the High Seas around the North East Atlantic to ensure the long-term conservation and sustainable economic, environmental, and social benefits from these important resources. This is crucial for pelagic stocks like ASH, mackerel & blue whiting that move between national and international waters.
Robust international management is essential for the long-term security of international fisheries, and these meetings offer decision makers another opportunity to take meaningful strides toward a sustainable future for NEA pelagic stocks.
NAPA will be attending this year’s meeting, and we will be putting forward our view on why the pathway to a sharing arrangement may not be as complicated as it might seem. Watch this space for further details.

The board that oversees the menhaden fisheries up and down the Atlantic Coast will reduce the catch limit for the species by 20% next year.
The Atlantic Menhaden Management Board, which is a part of the Atlantic States Marine Fisheries Commission (ASMFC), voted Oct. 28 to decrease the total allowable catch (TAC) to less than 190,000 metric tons, down from 230,000 metric tons set for the 2025 TAC.
