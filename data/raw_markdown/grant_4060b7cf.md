Mary Clark Thompson Grant
=========================
In keeping with founder Mary Clark Thompson’s spirit of community-minded philanthropy, the F.F. Thompson Foundation sponsors a Community Health Grants Program.  Through a competitive application process, grants of $500 to $2,500 are awarded to local community not-for-profit organizations that are committed to improving the health and wellness of residents and communities in UR Medicine’s Thompson Health service area.
**Application deadline: April 10, 2026.**
We look forward to your participation in our efforts to create caring partnerships in our community.
View guidelines and access online application below.  Final Report to be submitted by Dec. 1 of the year funding was received.
If you have question regarding the online application, send an email to our webmaster.

### 2025 Mary Clark Thompson Grant Recipients
### Bloomfield Public Library
**Project:**AED Purchase. With MCT funding as well as funding from other donors, the Bloomfield Public Library will purchase an AED.
### Budding Readers
**Project:** Provide books to UPK, Kindergarten, and 1st grade. Budding Readers will use the MCT funding to educate, encourage and equip parents to read with their young children as natural part of their daily routine, and hopes that their children will develop oral language and pre-literacy skills that will help them become skilled and fluent readers and develop a lifelong love of books and a passion for reading.  The program includes eight communities in Ontario County: Bloomfield, Canandaigua, Honeoye, Marcus Whitman, Midlakes, Naples, Red Jacket, and Victor.
### Canandaigua Churches in Action
**Project: Supply a Smile.** Canandaigua Churches in Action holds an ongoing monthly program that provides free dental care for those underinsured. This includes exams, x-rays, cleanings, fillings, and more.
### Canandaigua Churches in Action
**Project: Free Smile Day.** Canandaigua Churches in Action is holding its annual Free Smile Day in which dental professionals volunteer an entire day of free dental services.  MCT support would cover dental supplies.
### Canandaigua Emergency Squad
**Project: Video Scope for airway.** Funds from the grant will cover the cost of a replacement video. This project will help ensure that our providers have up-to-date tools to help patients who are having an airway problem and need a higher level of care to ensure their airway remains open with successful intubation.
### Canandaigua Fire Department
**Project: Resuscitation Equipment.** MCT funds will allow CFD to add a ResQ CPR device and portable suction unit to a piece of apparatus. This equipment can increase the likelihood of survival of a patient in cardiac arrest.
### Family Counseling Service of the Finger Lakes
**Project: Youth Connection.**Family Counseling Service of the Finger Lakes, Inc. would use MCT funding to provide an 8-week therapeutic support group focused on increasing connection and social supports for adolescents impacted by trauma, adversity, and other stressors.
### Family Promise of Ontario County
**Project: Homeless Shelter and Eviction Prevention.** MCT funding will assist with the general support of Family Promise’s mission to help families who are homeless or facing homelessness.
### Happiness House
**Project: AED Purchase.**With MCT funding, the Canandaigua location will purchase an AED.
### Hospeace House, Inc
**Project: End of Life Doula Training.** An MCT grant will support end-of-life doula training for volunteers and staff.
### LeTourneau Christian Camp
**Project: Davis Sports Facility**
### Mercy Flight Central
**Project: Whole Blood Project.** Mercy Flight would use MCT funding to help with the purchase of the Whole Blood Project.  By implementing the Whole Blood Project, the air flight crew can ensure it has the necessary equipment to enhance positive health outcomes.
### Partnership for Ontario County
**Project: Community Support Center – Suicide Prevention.** MCT funding will allow for the purchase of suicide prevention materials, which will be shared with the community.
### The Salvation Army
**Project: Golden Spot Senior Center.**The Golden Spot Senior Center welcomes 35 seniors each week where they participate in various activities.  The funds will support programming costs, such as coffee and snacks, board games, craft supplies, outings, and transportation costs.
