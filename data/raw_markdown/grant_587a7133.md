#### What types of grants are available for nonprofits in Delaware?
Funding for grants in Delaware comes from a variety of sources, including private foundations, state/local government agencies, the federal government, corporate funders, and associations. Here’s an overview of funders providing the largest number of grants:
* **Private Foundations:** 80 grants in Delaware are funded by private foundations targeting a large range of categories from education to the environment to literacy. Private foundations make up the largest category of funders.
* **State & Local Government:** Currently, 27 grants are available from state/local government agencies in Delaware. Focus areas across these grants typically include land/habitat conservation, art and culture, and farming.
* **Corporate Funders:** 20 corporate grants are available in Delaware. These grants often focus on community development and revitalization, food access and hunger, and affordable housing.
* **Federal Government:** 17 federal grants are posted within the state. These grants generally focus on rural development, health and medicine, and agriculture.
When considering which types of grants to pursue, keep in mind that government grants often have large awards but equally large compliance requirements. When considering foundations and corporate funders, make sure your project aligns with their mission and focus areas.
#### When is the best time to apply for grants in Delaware?
Based on currently available data, the best time to apply for grants in Delaware is in Q1, which holds the highest percentage of grant deadlines at 34.5%. #### What is the overall funding landscape in Delaware?
The nonprofit funding landscape for grants in Delaware has shown a large change in activity over the last few years. Based on IRS funding reports, grant funding increased by 45.13% from ~$578 million in 2021 to ~$839 million in 2022. The largest focus areas for funding included alliance/advocacy organizations, professional societies and associations, and research institutes and/or public policy analysis. The top three counties in Delaware that received the most funding during this time were New Castle County, Kent County, and Sussex County.
