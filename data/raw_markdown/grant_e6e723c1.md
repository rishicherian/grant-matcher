#### Summary
This grant opportunity supports projects that strengthen connections between agriculture, education, and local communities across the state of Vermont. The funding is intended to help organizations develop or expand initiatives that connect children and youth with local food systems, farms, and nutrition education. The overall purpose is to encourage programs that improve learning experiences while also promoting healthier food access and stronger community relationships tied to agriculture.
The program provides a pool of funding of about $275,000 to be distributed among several recipients. Individual grant awards typically range from approximately $10,000 to $60,000, depending on the size and scope of the project being proposed. In some cases, earlier rounds of funding have included similar ranges beginning around $5,000 and extending to larger project awards, allowing multiple organizations to participate in a single funding cycle. These funds are meant to support program development, partnerships, educational activities, and community-based efforts that encourage participation in agriculture-related learning and food initiatives.
The grant is available to applicants located within the state of Vermont and is intended for projects that directly benefit communities and youth throughout the region. Eligible applicants generally include nonprofit organizations, schools, school districts, afterschool programs, early childhood education providers, and community groups that work in collaboration with educational or community partners. While nonprofit organizations and educational institutions are the primary applicants, partnerships between organizations are often encouraged. Small businesses and private individuals are generally not the main recipients, although they may participate as collaborators or supporters within a broader project led by an eligible organization.
Funding is designed to support projects that expand educational opportunities and improve youth engagement with food, agriculture, and community resources. Activities may include creating or expanding programs that connect students with local farms, improving school or childcare access to locally sourced food, or developing educational activities that explore topics such as nutrition, environmental awareness, and local food systems. The grant also shows interest in projects that can be replicated in other communities, involve partnerships, and demonstrate meaningful impact on children and families.
Overall, the grant offers financial support to organizations working to strengthen community connections through agriculture-based learning and food initiatives in Vermont. By providing funding to expand programs and collaborations, the opportunity aims to improve youth education, community engagement, and access to healthy local food while supporting projects that can continue to benefit communities across the state over time.
