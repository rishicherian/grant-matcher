Current Applications
====================
2026 CDBG Applications
----------------------
The following applications were received in response to a Notice of Funding Availability for 2026 Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) funds. Applications are separated into Public Service and Development categories due to federal law that caps the amount of CDBG funding that can be spent on Public Service projects to 15% of the City's total allocation. The CDBG Advisory Board reviews non-CEDO applications during February, and March of 2026. Below are the results of the Advisory Board's recommended awards pending Mayor, City Council and HUD approval.
Public Service Applications
---------------------------
###
### Village Hydroponics
Project Application - Community Hydroponics & Winter Food Access Program
Program provides free, culturally relevant produce and hands-on food education to low- and moderate-income Burlington residents during the winter months, when food access barriers are highest. Through partnerships with The Family Room and Champlain Housing Trust, the program combines winter produce distributions with community-based hydroponic education to strengthen food security, nutrition, and community connection.
* Amount Requested: $13,000
* **Recommended Award: $10,000**
### Champlain Valley Office of Economic Opportunity (CVOEO)
Project Application - Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA)
Program provides free tax preparation and electronic filing services to households earning $69,000 or less through a network of community partnerships and IRS-certified volunteers. The program serves low- to moderate-income individuals, seniors, people with disabilities, limited-English-speaking New Americans, and their families by reducing barriers to essential financial services.
* Amount Requested: $16,000
* **Recommended Award: $13,500**
### Lund
Project Application - Lund's Parent Child Center and Clinical Treatment Services
Program offers therapeutic childcare, parenting and child development education, supervised visitation, and substance use and mental health treatment to families in Burlington. These programs are uniquely designed to work collaboratively, supporting children and families who are at risk of or have experienced Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs) such as mental illness, substance use, physical or emotional child abuse or neglect, violence in the home or community, and parental incarceration.
* Amount Requested: $40,000
* **Recommended Award: $20,000**
### Steps to End Domestic Violence
Project Application - Safe Tonight
Provide emergency housing and support services to adults and children fleeing domestic violence in their homes. Programming allows people experiencing domestic violence to have access to our advocates who can assist with finding and securing safe, permanent housing, increasing self-sufficiency and establishing economic independence.
* Amount Requested: $73,212
* **Recommended Award: $36,000**
### Pathways Vermont
Project Application - Permanent Supportive Housing Services in Chittenden County
Program provides permanent housing placement along with intensive community-based services to individuals with histories of homelessness and institutionalization, most of whom are living with disabling conditions. The program utilizes local housing stock to facilitate community reintegration.
* Amount Requested: $35,000
* **Recommended Award: $30,000**
###
Development Applications
------------------------
###
### CVOEO
Project Application - South End Food Hub
Rehabilitation of a 7,853 sq. ft. existing commercial space to accommodate a new food hub to be located at 255 S. Champlain St. - the heart of King Street neighborhood.
* Amount Requested: $142,000
* **Recommended Award: $142,000**
### Mercy Connections
Project Application - Small Business Equity Project
Project delivers high-quality entrepreneurial and English Language Learning (ELL) education to support the growth of small businesses in Vermont, with special focus on needs of underserved populations including people with marginalized gender identities, adults with different abilities, immigrants and refugees, and Black, Indigenous, and People of Color (BIPOC). It provides business and professional development support and training in ways that meet participants’ unique needs and remove barriers to their success.
* Amount Requested: $75,000
* **Recommended Award: $75,000**
### CVOEO
Project Application - Financial Futures: Micro Business Development Program
Helps LMI Burlington residents start, sustain, and expand micro businesses. Participants have access to coaching, classes, and technical assistance for topics including product development, business plans, budgeting, credit, marketing, pricing, taxes, bookkeeping, applying for funding, and expansion.
* Amount Requested: $72,761
* **Recommended Award: $72,761**
### South End Arts & Business Association (SEABA)
Project Application - Creative Microbusiness Incubator
Program supports LMI Burlington residents by providing them opportunities to grow their microbusinesses, in turn supporting Burlington’s growing Creative Sector. Participants will have subsidized access to an established brick and mortar space, business guidance, marketing, and community networking opportunities that will result in sustainable financial stability.
* Amount Requested: $28,450
* **Recommended Award: $28,450**
### Winooski Valley Park District (WVPD)
Project Application - Ethan Allen Homestead Museum Barn Renovation - Phase II
Project will include renovations at the Hill Brownell Educational Center – AKA the “Museum Barn”, located in the 294-acre Ethan Allen Homestead Park owned and operated by WVPD. The scope of work includes upgrade of the buildings’ HVAC systems (last replaced in 2002), replacement/upgrade of flooring (original - circa 1986), the replacement of aging dividers in the bathrooms (also original - circa 1986).
* Amount Requested: $75,164
* **Recommended Award: $75,164**
