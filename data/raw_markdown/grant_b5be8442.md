About the application
---------------------
Administered by Create Baltimore (formerly BOPA)
**This grant provides general operating support to Baltimore-based arts and cultural organizations with operating budgets under $300,000.** General operating funding helps strengthen daily operations, sustain creative programming, and increase community access to the arts. Organizations selected for funding will receive one general operating grant of either $5,000 or $10,000. The award amount is determined by the adjudicators.
Grants will be awarded in two funding cycles:
* **Cycle 1:** $10,000 grants distributed by March 6, 2026
* **Cycle 2:** $5,000 grants distributed by June 5, 2026
Organizations that have not received Creative Baltimore Fund general operating support in the past three fiscal years receive priority consideration. All applicants must demonstrate strong alignment with the program’s goals to be selected for funding.
***Estimated time to complete:** 60-90 minutes*
***Tip:** Have your budget, key staff information, and any recent photos or videos ready before you begin.*
About the event
---------------
Create Baltimore (CB) on behalf of the Mayor and the City of Baltimore, is excited to announce the Creative Baltimore Grant for FY 2026. Through this grant opportunity, CB grants funds to qualified artists and arts and cultural organizations based in Baltimore City. The annual allocation from the City’s general fund is administered by the Baltimore City Arts Council. The Creative Baltimore has two primary grant programs: General Operating Support (GOS) for organizations and the Mayor’s Individual Artist Award for project or practice support.

**ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA — Who May Apply**
To be eligible for the Creative Baltimore Fund's GOS grant program, applicants must meet each of the following criteria:
* Have a permanent and viable base in Baltimore City for a minimum of two years prior to the submission deadline for the FY26 GOS application.
* Be and have been a 501(c)(3) organization for at least two years OR be a successor to an arts or cultural organization that had been a 501(c)(3) tax exempt organization for at least three years prior to the submission deadline.
* Provide a Certificate of Good Standing certifying that the applicant is a registered nonprofit corporation, in good standing, in the State of Maryland.
* Have a primary mission to provide programs or activities in areas directly concerned with arts or culture\* for the general public.
*\*BOPA defines arts and culture as including, but not limited to, literature, theater, music, dance, fashion and fiber art, comedy and improv, food and culture, visual art (such as drawing, painting, sculpture, photography, film, etc.), placemaking (architecture, public art, murals, etc.), as well as history and natural sciences (archaeology, folk art, natural history, archving, etc.).*
Organizations that utilize fiscal sponsors are eligible. Additional requirements for fiscal sponsors:
* If the annual budget is $500,000 or more — Provide Independently Certified Audited Financial Statements for FY25 and FY24 (or the two most recent completed fiscal years);
* If the annual budget is less than $500,000 — Provide **either** Independently Certified Audited **or** Reviewed Financial Statements for FY25 and FY24 (or the two most recent completed fiscal years).
