Overview of EDGE Grants for Delaware Small Businesses
-----------------------------------------------------
EDGE Grants offer Delaware small businesses a competitive opportunity to secure significant funding, with STEM-based companies eligible for up to $100,000 and non-STEM Entrepreneur Class businesses up to $50,000.
These small business grants require a $3 state-to-$1 business matching fund, encouraging partnership between the state and applicants. EDGE Grants require businesses to match every $3 of state funds with $1 of their own investment.
Each application round awards up to 10 grants, split evenly between STEM and non-STEM businesses, via a competitive process managed by the Delaware Division of Small Business.
EDGE Grants began in 2019 and are funded through a cooperative agreement with the U.S. Small Business Administration, ensuring equal and nondiscriminatory access to **monetary aid**. Special consideration is often extended to businesses that uplift **disabled folks**, helping drive inclusive innovation across Delaware.
Antares REACH Grant Program Details and Eligibility
---------------------------------------------------
You’ll want to confirm your business meets the Antares REACH Grant eligibility, which requires majority ownership by historically underrepresented groups and a demonstrated need for growth funding.
The application process involves submitting detailed business information and completing a Business Health Score assessment.
Priority is often given to businesses in essential sectors like **housing services** and **health services**, reflecting their community impact. Winning the grant provides $20,000 in unrestricted capital plus access to mentorship, coaching, and community resources to help scale your business effectively.
### Grant Eligibility Criteria
The **Antares REACH Grant Program** requires businesses to be **for-profit entities** majority-owned (51% or more) by individuals from **historically underrepresented groups**, including womxn, people of color, military-affiliated persons, those with disabilities, or LGBTQ+ entrepreneurs.
To be eligible, your business must demonstrate growth potential with a solid **business plan** aligned to the grant’s goals. You also need to meet specific financial criteria and show how you’ll use funds effectively for **eligible expenses**.
Collaborating with partners to maximize impact within your community is encouraged, as the program supports sustainability throughout various market cycles.
### Application and Benefits
Although you may think applying will be complicated, the Antares REACH Grant Program streamlines the application process through an online portal where you create an account, complete a concise application form, and submit it before the deadline.
As a small business owner, you’ll benefit from grants per application round, typically $20,000, aimed at fueling growth. The program emphasizes private equity-backed middle-market companies and underrepresented entrepreneurs, requiring demonstrated funding needs and a scalable business model.
The grant is also inclusive of mission-driven organizations like **churches** and social enterprises led by **ex-felons**, helping expand opportunities for historically overlooked groups. Besides funding, you gain access to mentorship, peer networks, and market-cycle collaboration to guarantee your business thrives sustainably.

Frequently Asked Questions
--------------------------
### How Soon After Application Can Funds Be Expected if Awarded?
If awarded, you can typically expect funding disbursement within a few weeks after the application timeline closes and winners are announced, though exact timing varies by program and grant cycle in Delaware. Planning for about 2-4 weeks is common.
### Are Multiple Grants Allowed for the Same Business Over Time?
You can receive multiple grants over time if your business meets ongoing grant eligibility criteria and funding source rules. Each grant application is competitive and assessed independently, so repeated awards depend on your continued qualification and available funds.
### Can Grant Money Be Used for Employee Salaries or Benefits?
You can use grant money for employee compensation, including salaries and benefits, if the grant’s eligibility criteria explicitly allow it. Always review specific grant guidelines to guarantee compliance with employee compensation rules.
### What Reporting or Accountability Is Required From Grant Recipients?
You must meet specific **reporting requirements** by submitting receipts and documentation equal to your grant amount, along with tax returns and a CPA-certified revenue decrease letter if applicable. Failure triggers **accountability measures** like repayment or loan conversion.
### Is There Assistance Available for Businesses Without Accounting Expertise?
Yes, you can get assistance to improve your financial literacy and grant management skills, often through Delaware’s Small Business Development Center (SBDC) and related programs that provide coaching and technical support for grant recipients without accounting expertise.
