🎉 Meet Bianca Mauro and Michael Roundy: individual arts’ creators and co-founders of the Lowell Kinetic Sculpture Race!
Kinetic sculptures are engineered, wildly artistic, human-powered sculptures designed to travel over mud, sand, and water. Add “race” to the mix, and you have both the artist and their art put in motion—or better yet, put to the test!
We’ve caught up with both Bianca and Michael to learn how this typically West Coast and Mid-Atlantic Region competition came to Lowell, and how has hosting it in our mill city enriched its community.
Read more on our blog page on the Mosaic Lowell Website!

Points of Light Lantern Celebration is looking for volunteers for Saturday, May 2! This community event celebrates the many cultures that make Lowell special through food, music, art, and performances. The evening ends with hundreds of lanterns floating on the Western Canal. Volunteers help make the event possible by supporting things like:
• Event setup and breakdown
• Greeting and directing guests
• Helping with lantern decorating stations
• Assisting with activities and performances
It’s a fun way to connect with the Lowell community and support a meaningful cultural celebration. Shifts as early as 9 am and as late as 11 pm, flexible hours, great for kids or groups. Learn more and sign up at https://pointsoflightlowell.com/helpus

April is often logged as the soggy friend between the frosty month of spring (March) and the sunny month paving into summer (May). But have you ever considered April to be a month of transitions, as the stems are working through the hard-packed soil to bring you this year’s earliest blossoms, and the geese returning to nest as soon as the ice have melted? What about all the colors before that surge of green—pink and violet and egg-shell yellow, unfurling from winter’s recesses, shifting into a mosaic to last the rest of the year?
It’s as they say: April exhibits bring May exquisites! But let’s not cast April off to the side, which is a wonder in itself.
