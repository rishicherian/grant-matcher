Geographic Focus
----------------
Southern Maine, specifically:
* York County
* Cumberland County (excluding Brunswick and Harpswell)
Funding Priorities
------------------
United Way of Southern Maine aligns funding with their **Thrive2027** initiative focusing on three ten-year goals:
* **Kids getting a strong start** - Educational success and youth opportunity
* **Neighbors empowered to thrive** - Financial security and stability
* **People living longer, better lives** - Health and well-being
Specific areas of focus include:
* Child care access
* Food insecurity
* Housing instability
* Mental health services
* Community resilience
* Racial equity initiatives
Programs must demonstrate commitment to racial equity, collaboration, and sound fiscal management.
What They Don't Fund
--------------------
* General operating expenses
* Capital campaigns
* Previously incurred expenses
* Religious activities or services
* Promotion of religious beliefs
* Activities restricted to church or religious group membership
* Political activities or lobbying for specific candidates (advocacy for legislation aligned with Thrive2027 goals is allowed)
Application Method
------------------
**Community Investment Process** - Fixed deadline cycle, typically opening annually. The funding period July 2025-2027 application process is now closed.
**Additional Programs**:
* Capacity Building grants (information released December 2026)
* Giving Circles (Women United, Emerging Leaders United) - applications open Spring 2026
* Racial Equity Fund
Organizations must:
* Request minimum $10,000
* Serve residents of York or Cumberland County (minus Brunswick and Harpswell)
* Endorse Thrive2027's three ten-year goals
* Complete volunteer-led review process
