There are so many people arguing about a problem to admit a doctoral student in mathematics, which is strange. This only indicates that these people are psychologically twisted and unhealthy.
Admission to a PhD in mathematics is not necessarily a benefit. Researching mathematics is a job. Being admitted does not mean you will be paid to play; it means you will be tasked with completing work. Doing well will earn you a graduation certificate, which is just a piece of paper. This paper has no real use, unlike a computer graduation certificate, which can help secure high-paying jobs.
If someone is not interested in math, it's hard to understand why they would be envious. Assuming they were admitted, how could they do a good job if they don't have an interest? No matter whether the job is good or bad, doing research in math is more painful than working in an electronics factory. This job neither interests them nor is it easy, requiring them to do it as if they were in prison. Could they envy someone who can go to prison?
If someone is interested in mathematics, it is equally difficult for him to understand why he should be envious. He is interested in mathematics rather than attending a prestigious school; even if he doesn't get the opportunity, he should be happy being a folk mathematician. He won't come to focus on this issue, but rather he is already deeply immersed in the study and research of mathematics.
In the end, it is hard to understand why a normal person would be interested in this issue and engage in conversations with a stranger online. The only reasonable explanation is that there is a psychological distortion or a mental illness.
