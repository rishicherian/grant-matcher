Now in its fifth round, the FSIG program seeks to support food security initiatives that provide more access to locally grown, raised, harvested and caught foods, by strengthening the Commonwealth’s food supply chain.  **Applications are due by May 5, 2025 at 4:00pm. For more information and to apply, go to mass.gov/ApplytoFSIG.**  Launched in 2020 as a response to the pandemic and supply chain disruptions, the Commonwealth has continued the program under MDAR’s new Division of Food Security to build supply chain resilience by increasing the capacity of our farmers, fishers and other local and regional food system entities to feed the people of Massachusetts.  **Key Details:**   * FY26 awards will range from $10,000 - $1,000,000 requiring a 20% cash match. * Eligible applicants include Massachusetts’ farmers, fishers, food system entities, and public and Tribal entities. Applicants must contribute to the local food system and have been in operation for at least 3 years. * Proposals must align with one of the following four project types: expansion of agriculture & seafood production, local & regional mid-supply chain expansion, food access & availability, emergency preparedness & resilience. * Projects must be for capital improvements such as season extension equipment, cold storage and distribution expansion, value-added processing equipment, adaptive infrastructure that protects critical infrastructure against supply chain disruptions, equipment for increasing food production and more.   **Visit Mass.gov/ApplytoFSIG to find:**   * Request For Responses (RFR) and application materials. The RFR provides a comprehensive summary of grant evaluation criteria, review timelines, award recipient expectations, eligible uses of grant funds, examples of projects, and more. We highly encourage all interested applicants to read it in entirety. * Pre-recorded informational webinar, presentation slides and instructions on how to submit the application. * Q&A document which will be periodically updated.   **Questions: All questions must be submitted in writing to FSIGGrant@mass.gov by Monday, April 14, 2025, at 4:00 p.m.**  **Past Years FSIG Awardees: Share Your Success Story**  MDAR is seeking impact stories from past FSIG project awardees. Please complete this form if interested in sharing about your project’s impact and we may reach out to learn more.   |  | | --- | | --- | | | |  | |
