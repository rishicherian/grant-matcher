Proposals will be accepted on a rolling basis through September 2, 2025 at 2pm.    All development activities such as acquisition, construction, and rehabilitation of affordable rental housing in Erie County are eligible expenses, including soft costs. Single-family housing is not eligible under this RFP.      Developers, community development corporations, not-for-profits, and other qualified housing providers looking to expand the supply of quality affordable housing are encouraged to apply. This includes emerging developers with significant local community involvement.     An information meeting will be held via Webex on Wednesday July 23, 2025 at 10am. Please email Project Coordinator Monica Boutin at Monica.Boutin@erie.gov to register and receive a link for the meeting.      Proposals will be accepted on a rolling basis through September 2, 2025 at 2pm. The Josephine Goodyear Foundation's mission calls for assistance to non-profit agencies that help “indigent women and children, particularly with their physical needs.” In the past, most of the Foundation's efforts have been focused on medical facilities but the Foundation has broadened that scope in more recent years to aid disadvantaged women and children in other ways. Funding is concentrated on the Greater Buffalo area.    Applicants must be 501(c)3 nonprofit organizations. NY BRICKS Grants can be used to fund the acquisition, design, construction, major renovation, and equipping of a community center, along with other related capital costs.    Eligible applicants may be awarded grants between $250,000 and $15,000,000. The NY BRICKS Initiative will provide not less than $50 million in funding for community centers in underserved communities. NOTE: Grantees must pay for at least 20% of the capital costs of the project, called Matching Funds. Money contributed to the project after April 1, 2025 may be counted as Matching Funds. A NY BRICKS grant will only cover up to 80% of the capital costs for the proposed project.    Municipalities and not-for-profit entities providing community centers are eligible to apply. Not-for-profit entities must be Prequalified in the Statewide Financial System (SFS) before submitting an application to be eligible to apply. This process may take several weeks to complete. To view a prerecorded webinar on Prequalification in SFS, click here.    Applications will be accepted from July 14 through August 15, 2025 at 3pm. To view the RFP, click here.
