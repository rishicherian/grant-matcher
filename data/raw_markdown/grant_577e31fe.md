About us
--------
Anchored by the greater Rochester/Finger Lakes region, New York State is a hotbed for Optics, Photonics, Imaging, and Laser (OPIL) technology. With a history that dates back to the 1850s, the New York Photonics cluster is now more than 150 companies strong, with significant support from research universities, workforce development programs at community colleges, and state and federal economic development initiatives.
Our cluster’s expertise in OPIL technologies sparks breakthrough research, catalyzes product development across all advanced manufacturing sectors, and sustains one of the most sophisticated, highly integrated supply chains in the nation. Look to New York Photonics as the organization for problem-solving and business-building, with a collaborative ethic that maintains rigorous standards and understands the importance of speed to market.

Edited
Over the last few weeks, as I approached my two-year mark at QED Technologies, I’ve found myself reflecting on how far we’ve come, what we’ve built together, and where we are headed next.
I first came to know QED Technologies International LLC as a customer (albeit, on the sales side of things) and even had a chance in 2018 to give a talk to a group of customers on how we were using QED MRF at Thorlabs to make precision aspheres. Back then it was clear that QED stood for something special: deep technical excellence, enabling technology (MRF, ASI) and a brand associated with helping customers solve challenging production and metrology problems.
When I joined QED in 2024, I did so with enormous respect for its history, deep appreciation for the legacy of Rochester’s optics industry and a strong belief in its future.
Over the last two years, we have grown our team (more than 2X!), expanded our capabilities, broadened our global footprint, and continued investing in the people, technology, and infrastructure needed to build a stronger business for the future.
We have also evolved our strategy.
At QED, our future is not about moving away from the technologies that made us who we are. It is about building from that foundation. MRF, ASI, and NMF remain at the core of our business and our identity - MRF is our DNA!
At the same time, we are investing in a broader vision for the future of the precision optics ecosystem: a more complete suite of technologies, deeper customer partnerships, stronger manufacturing and service capabilities, and a continued commitment to making extreme precision more accessible and impactful for our customers and our industry.
That is what excites me most right now. We are helping shape an ecosystem where innovation in precision optics can move faster and enabling technologies can come together in more powerful ways.
Growth is never linear, and we’ve certainly had challenges along the way. But I am proud of how our team continues to respond: with resilience, humility, and a commitment to getting better. Strategy matters, but execution and follow-through are what define a company.
I’m incredibly grateful to the QED team, our leadership group, our partners, and our customers for the trust and commitment that have made these first two years so meaningful. Thank you all for your guidance and support these first two years.
I’m also personally grateful to the Rochester optics community and the greater Rochester, NY area as a whole. My family and I love it here. We’ve made amazing friends, felt truly welcomed, and have come to appreciate just how special this community is.
The future in front of us is compelling, and I could not be more energized about what we are building next.
We’re building what’s next, enabling the most advanced high precision applications across semiconductor, defense, space, laser fusion, and other mission critical, optics-enabled markets.
