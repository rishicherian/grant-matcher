Building a Resilient Newport: Explore Community Grant Funding
-------------------------------------------------------------
---
*To enhance residents' quality of life, ensure the responsible stewardship of public funds, and strengthen mission-driven partner organizations delivering measurable impact, the City of Newport's Grants Office administers two community-focused grant programs:
The application portal for the FY-27 Community Grants is now closed.*
**Annual Civic Support Grant Program   |    Special Projects Grant Program**
These programs are designed to strengthen Newport’s neighborhoods and improve quality of life—especially for low- and moderate-income individuals and families. Through targeted investments, the City supports initiatives that promote economic development, enhance public health, increase access to arts and culture, expand recreational opportunities, and foster overall community well-being. With an emphasis on equity, accountability, and transparency, the City is implementing a long-term digital strategy to improve the grant process.
---
##### Applicant Eligibility
Universal eligibility requirements for both the Annual Civic Support Grant and Special Projects Grant Funds will be applied to all applicants. **To be considered for either grant program, applicants must submit the following documents:**
1. A copy of the IRS 501C-(3) certification letter, A valid certificate of good standing from the RI Secretary of State’s Office
2. Roster of current board members with service terms and professional affiliations indicated
3. Most recent Audited financials for organizations with annual operating budgets OVER $500,000
4. Most recent review or compilation of financials for organizations with annual operating budgets UNDER $500,000
5. Documentation that organization is based in, and/or primarily serves residents of the City of Newport
**INELIGIBILE APPLICANTS**
- For-profit organizations, businesses, or sole proprietors
- Educational institutions including private or public K-12 schools, colleges, universities, and community colleges
- Organizations with delinquent Final Grant Reports from previous awards

##### Evaluation Criteria
*All applicants are evaluated on the basis of organizational capacity and programmatic impact & value. The five key organizational areas include:*
**1. Mission** – Applicant clearly demonstrates its mission’s value to the Newport community. High-scoring applications will show how the organization fulfills an identified community need, and effectively serves residents.
**2. Programs & Services-**Applicant will demonstrate how its programs and services advance the organization’s stated mission, and uphold organization’s values.
**3. Community Impact & Population Served -** Applicant will demonstrate how its programs/services fulfill a community need identified through the City’s Comprehensive Plan.
**4. Management & Governance -** Applicant will provide evidence of effective management, governance, and strategic planning.
**5. Financial Management -**Applicant will provide evidence of sound fiscal management and policy.
*In addition to the five organizational capacity criteria, applications are scored by Six Programmatic Criteria related to the specific project or funder’s requirements, including:*
**▪ Project Need / Problem Statement
▪ Project Goals and Objectives
▪ Evaluation Plan
▪ Sustainability Plan
▪ Budget Plan & Worksheet Detail
▪ Partnerships**
##### SMART Goals & Evaluation Chart
SMART Goals are:
o **Specific** – Clearly define what is to be achieved.
o **Measurable** – Establish criteria to track progress and determine success.
o **Achievable** – Ensure the goal is realistic and attainable.
o **Relevant** – Align the goal with broader priorities or needs.
o **Time-bound** – Set a deadline or timeframe for completion.
|  |  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Criteria** | **4 – Excellent** | **3 – Good** | **2 – Fair** | **1 – Poor** |
| **Project Need / Problem Statement** | Problem is clearly defined, compelling, and well-supported by data or evidence. | Problem is clear and supported, somewhat general. | Problem is somewhat vague; limited supporting evidence. | Problem is unclear or unsupported. |
| **Project Goals\* and Objectives** | Goals are SMART and clearly linked to the problem. | Goals are mostly SMART and relevant. | Goals are somewhat vague not fully SMART. | Goals are unclear or not measurable. |
| **Project Implementation Plan** | Plan is highly detailed, feasible, and includes timeline and budget. | Plan is clear with most necessary components. | Plan is basic, missing details or lacks clarity. | Plan is incomplete, unrealistic, lacks key elements. |
| **Evaluation Plan** | Evaluation approach is thorough, logical, and linked to goals. | Evaluation is clear and measurable. | Evaluation is vague or lacks detail. | Evaluation is missing or poorly defined. |
| **Sustainability Plan** | Strong plan for long- term sustainability and future funding. | Evidence of reasonable sustainability approach. | Sustainability is mentioned but underdeveloped. | No clear sustainability plans. |
| **Budget** | Budget is detailed, justified, and aligns well with the project plan. | Budget is mostly clear and aligned. | Budget is somewhat vague or lacks justification. | Budget is unrealistic, unclear, or misaligned. |
| **Partnerships** | Strong, relevant partnerships clearly described. | Partnerships exist and are moderately strong. | Limited or informal partnerships. | No evidence of partnerships. |
| **Organizational Capacity** | Organization has strong experience, resources, and expertise. | Organization is capable with relevant experience. | Capacity is adequate but may have gaps. | Organization lacks demonstrated capacity. |

**Proposal Review Process
Community & Peer Panel Review**
▪ Eligible applications will be referred to a competitive review process led by R&S staff and comprised of volunteer panel of professionals assembled from appropriate community areas and City Departments
▪ The review panel uses a rubric to evaluate and score proposals
**Funding Recommendations**
▪ The panel provides award funding recommendations to R&S to forward to council
▪ Award & funding recommendations are reviewed and potentially revised by City Council
▪ Following the City’s adoption of a final budget, applicants will be notified of funding decisions and award disbursement
