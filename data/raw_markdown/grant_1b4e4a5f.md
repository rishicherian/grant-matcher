**Update to Pre-proposal Conference information:** ***The thresholds for the Buy America Build America (BABA) requirements were misstated in the Pre-Proposal Conference on June 9 and the Q/A document uploaded on June 23. Slide 10 incorrectly noted three thresholds for BABA. The correct information is: if a proposed project has a total development cost of $250,000 and any level of Federal funding (including HOME or CDBG), other than pandemic-related sources such as ARPA or HOME-ARP, then BABA applies. The applicability of the number of units in a project was stated correctly.***
***For further details on BABA requirements, please refer to the RFP as well as the Federal regulations linked in the RFP as well as on this page’s resources.***
Introduction/Overview
---------------------
The City of Rochester, New York, is seeking proposals from developers, non-profits, and housing service providers (Developers) to increase the supply of high-quality, safe, affordable rental and ownership housing opportunities within the geographical limits of the city of Rochester.  Developers may respond to this RFP to competitively apply for gap financing, a Payment-in-Lieu-of-Taxes (PILOT) agreement, purchase of City-owned real estate, and/or an official City letter of support for assistance from New York State Homes and Community Renewal (NYS HCR), New York State Housing Finance Agency (HFA), NYS Affordable Homeownership Opportunities Program (AHOP) or Block by Block, or other agencies and funding sources.  Projects will be ranked and then scored with “like” projects in, as well as in terms of funding structure (ex.: 9% versus 4% LIHTC).**This year, Developers may additionally respond to this RFP to apply to the City of Rochester for HOME-ARP funding.** The primary goal of the City’s HOME-ARP program is to increase the supply of housing for households experiencing homelessness or other eligible Qualified Populations (QP), as defined by HUD HOME-ARP Notice CPD-21-10.  This includes a number of populations experiencing housing instability, described further in **Part 6 - HOME-ARP**.  Developers may apply for HOME funding, HOME-ARP funding, or a combination of the two.
