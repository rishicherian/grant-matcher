--------
Pitt’s Clinical and Translational Science Institute (CTSI) transforms scientific research and clinical discoveries into modern medical techniques that improve lives. Our staff work directly with researchers in collaboration with regulatory agencies to navigate all necessary regulatory pathways, at any stage of their research.

🏥 Not all aneurysms behave the same — so why are they treated that way?
Aneurisk Inc., a Pitt BioE startup led by Nathan Liang, Timothy Chung, David Vorp, and Micah Guffey, is working to move beyond one-size-fits-all risk models by combining medical imaging, biomechanics, and artificial intelligence to deliver personalized insights for arterial aneurysm care. By helping clinicians better assess rupture risk, their platform aims to support more informed, timely, and life-saving decisions.
Built on decades of pioneering research in vascular biomechanics, Aneurisk is bringing a new level of precision to how aneurysms are understood and managed in clinical practice.
We’re proud to celebrate Aneurisk Inc. on Pitt Startup Signing Day and the future of personalized care they’re helping advance!

🎉 Congratulations to the five Pitt Health Sciences faculty members selected for the 2025 Ascending Star Awards — with a special shoutout to two outstanding members of the Pitt CTSI community!

⭐ Dr. William Evans, a 2019 Pitt Innovation Challenge (PInCh) winner, will present on Friday, November 7. His ongoing work in communication sciences reflects the innovative spirit that defines the PInCh community.
